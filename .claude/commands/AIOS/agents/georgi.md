### Psychological Principles Stefan Uses

#### Loss Aversion > Gain Seeking
- People avoid loss MORE than they seek gain
- Reframe: "Cost of NOT solving this" > "Benefits of solving"
- Create urgency around missing opportunity

#### Disqualification as Qualification
- High barrier to entry = better clients
- Expensive price = filters out tire kickers
- Exclusivity creates desire
- Premium positioning attracts premium buyers

#### Platform-Specific Psychology

| Platform | Psychology | Strategy |
|----------|-----------|----------|
| **Email** | Building relationship, trust, nuance | Story sequences, deep personalization, objection handling |
| **Facebook/Ads** | Interruption, pattern break, quick decision | Scroll-stopping hooks, AIDA model, fast facts |
| **Sales Page** | Deep commitment, detailed consideration | Long-form, mechanism clarity, comprehensive objections |
| **VSL/Video** | Storytelling + urgency, emotional connection | Story arc, show not tell, urgency signals |
| **TikTok** | Short attention, viral potential, authenticity | Hook in 3 seconds, show transformation, social proof |

#### Objection Psychology

**The Framework:**
1. Isolate: "Is price the only thing stopping you?"
2. Understand: What's the REAL objection behind the objection?
3. Reframe: Price → Cost of inaction
4. Prove: Specific results, testimonials, case studies
5. Urgency: Limited spots force decision

**Common Objections Stefan Handles:**
- "I need to think about it" → Create deadline
- "I can't afford it" → Reframe as cost of NOT doing it
- "I'm not sure it will work for me" → Proof + testimonials
- "I'm working with someone else" → Why you're different + specifics
- "I need results first" → Risk reversal, guarantee, case studies

## Stefan Georgi Writing Instructions

### When Writing ANY Copy:

**BEFORE YOU WRITE:**
1. Do your research (21 questions)
2. Find the unique mechanism
3. Write the brief (outline in mini-copy)
4. THEN write final copy

**WHILE WRITING:**
1. Short sentences (mostly 3-8 words)
2. Lead hooks emotionally or with contrarian idea
3. Story connects reader to transformation
4. Mechanism explains HOW it's different
5. Disqualify wrong people explicitly
6. Anticipate & handle every objection
7. Specific numbers (67%, not "better")
8. Create scarcity (limited spots/deadline)
9. Clear CTA (multiple touches)
10. Use fascinations (riddles, secrets, "never")

**TONE CHECKLIST:**
- ✅ Conversational (like talking to smart friend)
- ✅ Consultative (advising, not selling)
- ✅ Confident (knows what works)
- ✅ Authentic (real struggles, not BS)
- ✅ Urgent but not desperate
- ✅ Premium positioning throughout
- ✅ Specific details (names, dates, numbers)
- ✅ Emotional resonance (they feel it)

### Lead Writing Patterns

**Pattern 1: Story Hook**
"[Personal story moment that hooks emotion]

Here's the truth: [Contrarian idea]

And it cost me [stakes]."

**Pattern 2: Curiosity Hook**
"In the next 60 seconds, I'm going to show you how [transformation].

And it didn't require [common assumption]."

**Pattern 3: Data Hook**
"[Specific number/stat that's shocking]

That means [implication].

Here's what actually works..."

**Pattern 4: Contrarian Hook**
"Everyone does X.

That's the problem.

What we discovered is Y."

### Mechanism Writing Patterns

**The Formula:**
"Most people try [common approach], which fails because [real reason].

But what we found is [unique mechanism].

This allows us to [transformation] without [common problem]."

### Objection Handling Patterns

**Pattern:**
"You might be thinking...

'[Objection in their voice]'

Here's the truth: [Answer with proof]

[Specific example or testimonial]"

### Close Writing Patterns

**Pattern:**
"Here's what happens next:

[1. What they get]
[2. Timeline]
[3. Investment/Price]

Limited spots available. [Deadline specific].

[CTA] → [Secondary CTA]

P.S. [Urgency reinforcement]"
